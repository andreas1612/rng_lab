# Legal Framework & Disclaimer Language

## Service Definition

Finalogic's Pre-Audit RNG Readiness Tool provides a **technical readiness assessment** —
not a certified audit. This distinction is the foundation of the entire legal framework.

---

## What the Service Is

The service applies open-source, publicly available statistical test suites (NIST SP 800-22,
Dieharder, PractRand) to a client-submitted RNG output file, scores the results against
publicly published regulatory thresholds for selected jurisdictions, and produces a
Pre-Audit Readiness Report summarising the findings.

The service is equivalent in legal category to a **pre-submission technical review** or
**mock gap analysis** — a class of advisory service that exists in many regulated industries
and does not require the advisor to hold the relevant operating license.

---

## What the Service Is NOT

The service is explicitly NOT any of the following:

1. **Not an accredited laboratory assessment.** Finalogic is not accredited under
   ISO/IEC 17025 (testing laboratories) or ISO/IEC 17020 (inspection bodies).

2. **Not a regulatory submission.** The Pre-Audit Readiness Report cannot be submitted
   to any gambling authority (MGA, UKGC, Spillemyndighed, CGA, or any other) as evidence
   of RNG compliance. It has no regulatory standing.

3. **Not a guarantee of audit passage.** A PASS result in this tool does not mean the
   client will pass a formal audit. Formal audits involve proprietary methodologies,
   additional evidence requirements, documentation review, and the auditor's professional
   judgment — none of which this tool replicates.

4. **Not legal advice.** Nothing in the report or tool constitutes legal advice.

5. **Not a certification.** No certificate, licence, or credential is issued.

## Mandatory Disclaimer Block

Every report generated by this tool MUST include the following block, verbatim, on the
first page and the last page:

---

> **IMPORTANT NOTICE — PLEASE READ**
>
> This Pre-Audit Readiness Report has been produced by Finalogic using open-source
> statistical test suites applied to RNG output data submitted by the client.
>
> **This report is NOT an accredited audit.** Finalogic is not an ISO/IEC 17025-accredited
> testing laboratory and is not approved by any gambling regulatory authority as an
> audit service provider.
>
> **This report does not constitute regulatory compliance evidence** and cannot be
> submitted to any gambling authority (including but not limited to the Malta Gaming
> Authority, UK Gambling Commission, Spillemyndigheden, or any Canadian provincial
> regulator) in support of a licence application or renewal.
>
> **A PASS result in this report does not guarantee that the submitted RNG will pass a
> formal accredited audit.** Accredited audits involve additional evidence, documentation
> review, and professional judgment beyond the scope of this tool.
>
> This report is provided for informational and internal preparedness purposes only.
> By accepting this report, the client confirms they have read and agreed to Finalogic's
> Acceptable Use Policy (AUP).

## Liability Limitation

The report and tool are provided "as is" for informational purposes. Finalogic accepts no
liability for:

- Regulatory decisions made by any authority following review of this report or any
  related formal audit
- Financial loss arising from reliance on this report
- Any formal audit outcome, whether pass or fail
- Actions taken by the client based on this report's findings

---

## Directors' Note on AUP Enforcement

The AUP (see AUP.md) must be agreed to by the client before a report is generated.
The tool should require a checkbox acknowledgement before the "Generate Report" action
is available. The AUP agreement timestamp and client identifier must be logged and
included in the generated report as a footer reference.

---

## Jurisdiction-Specific Caveats

Each jurisdiction section of the report must carry the following note:

> The thresholds applied for [JURISDICTION] are based on publicly available published
> standards as of [REPORT DATE]. Regulatory requirements may change. The client is
> responsible for verifying current requirements with the relevant authority or an
> approved audit service provider.
